The Points System

Unlike American sports leagues, the Premier League championship is not determined by a playoff. Instead, teams play 38 games between August and May, once against every team at home and away. Teams are ranked in a table by how many total points they have. Each game there can be three points given for a win, a single point for a draw, and zero points for a loss. If teams are tied on points, the team with the higher goal differential (total goals scored - total goals conceded) will have the higher league position.

Wins

The winning team recieves three points

Draws

Points are shared. Each team walks away with one point each

Losses

The losing team gets no points



Champions and Relegation

The Premier league title is given to the club at the top of the league table at the end of the season. In world football, there is a system of promotions and relegation. The bottom three clubs in the table are relegated (demoted) to the next division, and then three top teams from the division below are promoted and replace the relegated sides.

Competitions

What events do teams participate in each season?

  • The Premier League

    The top flight league of English football occurs between August and May, with twenty competitors.

  • FA Cup

    This is the oldest domestic cup competition in the world, with its first iteration in 1871. More than 700 teams in England and Wales compete in this tournament each season.

  • EFL Cup

    Similar to the FA Cup, this is a multi-league competition but only the 92 teams across the top four divisions compete in this tournament.

  • UEFA Champions League

    The top four placing Premier League teams from the prior season qualify for this tournament, playing against the best teams from other European leagues.

Arsenal

"The Gunners"

Founded in 1886, this team is known for their "Invincible" season in 2003/04, going undefeated in the EPL, the only team to do so.

  • Notable Players: Thierry Henry, Dennis Bergkamp, Ian Wright, Mesut Ozil, Bukayo Saka
  • Stadium: Emirates Stadium

Chelsea

"The Blues"

This club was organized in 1905. They are known for their record of the least goals conceded in a Premier League Season (15) in 2004/05.

  • Notable Players: Frank Lampard, Didier Drogba, Eden Hazard, N’Golo Kante, Peter Čech
  • Stadium: Stamford Bridge

Liverpool

"The Reds"

This Merseyside club was founded in 1892. One not too well-known fact about this team is they were the first English club to have a shirt logo sponsor, being Hitachi in 1975.

  • Notable Players: Kenny Dalglish, Michael Owen, Steven Gerrard, Mohammed Salah, Virgil Van Dijk
  • Stadium: Anfield Stadium

Manchester United

"The Red Devils"

This Manchester side was organzied in 1878 as Newton Heath LYR Football Club before the change to Manchester United in 1902. In 1999, they were the first team to win the Treble (Premier League, FA CUP, and UEFA Champions League) in the same season.

  • Notable Players: David Beckham, Paul Scholes, Rio Ferdinand, Cristiano Ronaldo, Marcus Rashford
  • Stadium: Old Trafford

Manchester City

"The Citizens"

Originally founded as St Mark's in 1880, this northwestern side become known by its current name, Manchester City in 1894. Manchester City is known for having the highest points finish in a Premier League season, with 100 in their 2017/18 campaign.

  • Notable Players: Colin Bell, Sergio Aguero, David Silva, Kevin De Bruye, Erling Haaland
  • Stadium: Etihad Stadium

Tottenham Hotspur

"The Lilywhites"

This London club was organized in 1882. Almost two decades later, in 1901, Spurs became the first and only non-league team to win the historic FA Cup.

  • Notable Players: Jimmy Greaves, Gary Linker, Jermaine Defoe, Harry Kane, Son Heung-min
  • Stadium: Tottenham Hotspur Stadium
